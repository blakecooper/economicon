Economicon

Rules Document v. 0.1
Updated 2019-10-28

Welcome to Economicon!

When playing Economicon, you assume the role of the mightiest of all bureaucrats: the central banker. The responsibility is yours, and yours alone, to ensure that your nation's economy is strong and stable. Yet, despite the awesome power you wield, your challenges are many, and your tools are few.

A game of Economicon lasts ten rounds. Each round, you'll strategically set your nation's monetary policy, encourage production of goods from various sectors of your economy, then arrange to sell those goods on the global market, all while contending with meddling lawmakers whose laws may or may not help you achieve your goals. You'll also have to decide how (or whether) to work with bankers from other nations to respond to chaotic global events, the outcomes of which could usher in a new era of prosperity for your nation... or ruin it!

At the end of ten rounds, the winner of Economicon is the player who has amassed the most valuable assets in their nation's reserve bank... provided their economy isn't too volatile! You may receive volatility tokens if your nation's citizens are unemployed, resources become scarce, or you break the law. No player can win the game with three or greater volatility tokens.

How to play

Before play begins

Each player puts XXXX into reserves. XXXX is placed in the open market. Each player places a unit of gold on their peg counter, as each player begins the game on a gold standard.

Shuffle the global events and law cards.

Rounds

Winning the game

After ten rounds, everybody adds up the value of all holdings in their reserves. Unlike when making purchases, every banker uses gold as the payment unit in the numerator of the value function when determining their reserves' final value.

The player has the most valuable economy of all players without possessing greater than three volatility tokens is the winner.

Definitions

Commodities

There are three commodities in Economicon: grain, oil and tech. Commodities are produced by nations, and bought and sold on the open market. A nation's population also consumes commodities each turn. Commodities can be used as a currency peg.

Consumption Requirement

Each round, the citizens of your nation will consume a number of commodities from your central reserve bank equal to the number of laws in effect. When commodities are consumed in this way, discard them from play.

Currency

Currency is used by nations to make purchases. Its value is determined either by the value of the unit to which it is "pegged," or by its own scarcity on the market (if no peg is determined). Currency can also be bought and sold in the marketplace, and a player can use another player's currency as their currency's peg.

Currency Peg

Global Events

Gold

Gold works a little differently than commodities or currency. It is mined, not created, meaning its supply is more stable. Gold counts as a universal currency, meaning every player may use their supply of gold to pay for their purchases. Players can also peg their currency to the value of gold. The banker with the msot gold in reserves gets to go first whenever order of play matters. At the end of the game, gold is the unit of currency used to determine the value of your reserve holdings.

Laws

Mining

At the beginning of each round, every nation mines gold by rolling a d6, and placing that number of new units of gold into their reserves.

Native currency

Native currency refers to the currency your nation uses to make purchases. Each nation has its own standard currency it can use for this purpose. Any nation can also use gold to make purchases. Other currencies and even commodities may also be used as native currency under special circumstances (when stated).

Order of play

When relevant (e.g., during the trading phase), players take turns in order based on who has the most gold in reserves.. Ties are broken by rolling dice.

Production

Production Cost

The cost to produce any commodity is equal to half the commodity's value on the open market.

Production Requirement

Each round during the production phase, your population must be put to work producing at least one of each type of commodity per law in effect. You must be able to pay all production costs to meet this requirement.

Reserve bank

Rewards and penalties

If during a given round you complied with both your nation's production and consumption requirements, and its laws, you may add one gold to your reserves and remove one volatility token. Otherwise, gain a volatility token.

Resolving the global event

The final action in a given round, determine whether the players were successful in achieving the goal stated on the card. Enact the "Success" or "Failure" outcomes as appropriate.

Trading

Unit

A unit refers to one token of any type of currency, commodity or gold.

Value

Volatility token
